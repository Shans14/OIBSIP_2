CHADWICK AARON BOSEMAN
1976-2020


Biography of Chadwick Aaron Boseman

Chadwick Aaron Boseman was an African-American actor and playwright whose talents and endearing personality won the hearts of millions of fans worldwide. He was born on November 29, 1976, in Anderson, South Carolina He graduated from T.L. Hanna High School in 1995 and attended Howard University in Washington, D.C. Boseman attended the Oxford Summer Program of the British American Drama Academy at Balliol College, Oxford.

Boseman also began appearing on television, beginning with a guest spot in 2003 on the police series Third Watch.He also briefly acted on the soap opera All My Children in a role later assumed by Michael B. Jordan. After several more plays, including “Deep Azure,” his best-known one, he moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in film. He got a recurring role in the T.V. series “Lincoln Heights”. He pursued film roles, and in 2008 he made his big-screen debut, appearing in the football biopic The Express. Boseman began playing prince T’Challa, also known as Black Panther, in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. His first film as the character was “Captain America: Civil War.” By 2018, he starred in “Black Panther,” a film that brought him international recognition and critical acclaim. The film was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Picture, making it the first superhero film to achieve said nomination. Boseman reprised his role as Black Panther for “Avengers: Infinity War” (2018) and “Avengers: Endgame” (2019).

Boseman appeared in many more films, including “Da 5 Bloods” and “Ma Rainey’s Black Bottom,” which earned him a Golden Globe Best Actor Award and a spot on “Time” magazine’s 10 Best Movie Performances of 2020. On August 28, 2020, news of Boseman’s death shook the world. He had been diagnosed with stage III colon cancer in 2016, and it progressed to stage IV in 2019. Before his death, Boseman had gone through several surgeries and chemotherapy. He gave this world something intangible, something immortal, and his game-changing legacy will forever remain.
